Framework and benchmark

AgriManager

A Framework and Benchmark for LLM-RL Generalization in Agricultural Management.

AgriManager asks whether prompt-conditioned LLM-RL generalizes across the heterogeneity of agricultural management: distributional shift within a fixed schema and structural shift across schemas.

Motivation

Agricultural RL faces distributional and structural shift.

The heterogeneity an agricultural RL policy faces decomposes into two qualitatively different axes. The first is distributional shift within a fixed interface: weather, soil, crop variety, or prices change while the observed variables, available actions, and management objective stay the same.

The second is structural shift across interfaces: different farms expose different observed variables, support different management actions, or optimize different objectives. We name this broader structural axis schema shift.

Axis 1

Distributional shift

The policy-facing schema is held fixed while the episode context distribution changes.

Axis 2

Schema shift

The policy-facing interface changes through the observation schema, action schema, or reward schema.

Policy class

Prompt-conditioned control

Prompt-conditioned LLM policies re-read the schema from the prompt of each new episode.

Framework

A schema-aware text interface over DSSAT, WOFOST, and Cycles.

01 Gym-wrapped simulators

DSSAT, WOFOST, and Cycles are accessed through gym-DSSAT, WOFOST-Gym, and CycleGym.

02 Structured schema prompt

The framework declares the policy-facing schema to the policy through a structured prompt template.

03 Policy updates

Policy updates are computed by VeRL; fixed-interface neural baselines are used for controlled comparison where applicable.

Benchmark

A three-tier generalization ladder.

The AgriManager benchmark decomposes generalization into eight settings, with a shared evaluation protocol.

T1

Distributional shift

Tier 1 holds the schema fixed and varies the episode context distribution.

  • Weather-regime shift
  • Cross-crop trait shift
  • Price-regime shift
T2

Single-axis schema shift

Tier 2 changes exactly one of the observation, action, or reward schema axes.

  • Observation-schema shift
  • Action-menu shift
  • Reward-formulation shift
T3

Compositional shift

Tier 3 changes multiple schema components together; T3.1 additionally changes simulator dynamics.

  • Cross-simulator transfer
  • Unified policy evaluation

Findings

What the benchmark shows.

01

Within fixed schemas, prompt-conditioned LLM-RL matches or exceeds NN-RL.

The paper reports this result on all three Tier 1 settings.

02

Reasoning becomes necessary when reward shape penalizes input use.

In the reported fixed-schema settings, explicit reasoning is required when the reward shape penalizes input use.

03

Single-axis schema shift imposes different burdens along each axis.

The reported burdens are information sufficiency for observation schema, joint action-menu coverage for action schema, and objective coverage for reward schema.

04

Unified prompt-conditioned control has a conditional positive result.

Under compositional schema shift, the paper reports generalization when training provides marginal coverage of each schema-axis component and each simulator whose dynamics it must cover.

Abstract

Formal summary.

Agricultural management is heterogeneous along two axes. The first is distributional shift within a fixed task interface: the same sensors, action menu, and objective, but with weather, planted crops, and prices varying across years and locations. The second is structural shift across interfaces: different farms expose different observed variables, available actions, and management objectives.

AgriManager formalizes the second axis as schema shift and unifies DSSAT, WOFOST, and Cycles under prompt-based schema specification. On this platform, the paper designs a three-tier generalization ladder: Tier 1 within-schema distributional shift, Tier 2 single-axis schema shift, and Tier 3 compositional schema shift across simulators.

Limitations

Simulator benchmark, not farm advice.

All evaluation is in simulation. DSSAT, WOFOST, and Cycles are mechanistic crop simulators without real sensor noise, actuator latency, or farmer-in-the-loop delays, so the paper's generalization claims hold at the simulator level only. Released policies should not be used to schedule inputs or planting decisions without local agronomic review and field validation.
